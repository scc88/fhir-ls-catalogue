Individual Case Safety Report

The FHIR standard for pharmacovigilance and adverse event reporting. ICSR replaces E2B(R3) XML with structured, real-time safety data that flows seamlessly between sponsors, regulators, and healthcare providers — ensuring patient safety signals are detected and acted on faster.

What is ICSR?

Safety Reporting, Modernized

FHIR ICSR transforms adverse event reporting from batch-uploaded XML files to real-time, structured safety data that integrates with clinical and regulatory systems natively.

🚨

Real-Time Signal Detection

Safety reports flow in real time — enabling faster signal detection and reducing the risk of delayed adverse event awareness.

🏥

EHR Integration

Adverse events reported directly from clinical systems, pre-populated with patient and medication data from the EHR.

🌐

E2B(R3) Compatibility

Full mapping to ICH E2B(R3) data elements ensures backward compatibility while enabling FHIR-native workflows.

📊

Aggregate Reporting

Support for PSURs, DSURs, and aggregate safety summaries alongside individual case reports.

🔗

Linked to ePI

Safety data cross-referenced to product labelling — enabling automated SmPC updates when safety signals emerge.

🤖

Automated Triage

Machine-readable data enables automated seriousness assessment, duplicate detection, and causality evaluation.

Why Implement ICSR?

Protect Patients, Faster

FHIR ICSR eliminates the lag in safety reporting — getting critical adverse event data to the right people, at the right time.

✓
Days to Minutes

Reduce initial case report submission time from days to minutes with structured, real-time exchange.

✓
Eliminate Re-Keying

EHR-populated reports eliminate manual data entry — reducing errors and improving data quality.

✓
Global Compliance

Meet ICH E2B(R3) requirements while future-proofing for FHIR-native regulatory exchange.

✓
Signal-to-Action

Structured data powers automated aggregate analysis — detect, confirm, and act on safety signals faster.
