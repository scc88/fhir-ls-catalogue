Overview

The Regulatory Tracking of Questions (RTQ) FHIR Implementation Guide defines a structured, machine-readable format for the exchange of questions from regulatory bodies to applicants (and vice versa) during the assessment of marketing authorisation applications.

Today, regulatory questions and responses are exchanged as unstructured PDF documents or in proprietary e-submission portals. RTQ replaces this with FHIR Questionnaire and QuestionnaireResponse resources, enabling automated case management, real-time tracking, and full audit traceability.

The standard is being developed collaboratively by the HL7 Biomedical Research & Regulation (BR&R) Work Group with active participation from EMA, Health Canada, and industry partners.

Use Cases

📝

Day 120 / Day 180 List of Questions

Structured delivery of assessment questions at defined milestones with machine-readable section references.

🔄

Responses to Outstanding Issues

Applicant responses linked back to original questions with references to supporting documents.

📊

Assessment Tracking & Metrics

Real-time dashboards on question-response cycles, time-to-response, and outstanding items.

🏛️

Multi-Agency Coordination

Harmonised Q&A format across multiple regulatory agencies reduces applicant burden.
